The repertoire

Workshop topics to choose from

Booking Amera for your studio or event? Choose from her repertoire below — most run as choreography or technique intensives (minimum time applies to some).

Egyptian Classics & Golden Era
BB & above

The Golden Era

Cinematic style of the 60s & 70s, with a focus on Souad Hosny and the choreographers who paved the way.

BB & above

Majensi — Grand Entrances

Opening entrance pieces of the great Egyptian dancers and how to make an impact on entrée.

BB & above

Om Koulsoum Revisited

A thousand feelings, light on technique — bring "Leesa Fakir" back to the stage.

Folkloric & Street Styles
BB & above

Cairo Streets — Shaabi

Explore Shaabi and its evolution — a way of living in Egypt, not just a style.

BB & above

Talking Taksim & Being Baladi

The differences, the overlaps, and the freedom of dancing Baladi and to a taksim.

All levels

Modern Saidii

A powerful choreography to a popular Egyptian song. Stick (Asaya) required.

Lebanese, Khaleeji & Beyond
BB & above

Lebanese Pop

Fast, fun and flirty — a Najwa Karam choreography with a touch of Dabke.

All levels

The Khaleej Meets Pop

Light, modern Khaleegy moves that still make you feel like an Arabic pop princess.

Beginners+

Greek Belly Dance

Zorba, Zimbekkico and shoulder shimmies — put a little "Oppa!" into your dance.

Musicality & Connection
BB & above

Drum Solo — Egyptian Style

Make the tablah talk with you — expression, technique and the dancer/drummer dialogue.

BB & above

Romantic Orientale

Fall in love with your dance — choreography to a romantic Arabic song by Wael Kfoury.

BB & above

Franco-Arabic

Revisiting a time in Arabic music where East meets West — Arabic with French, Italian and English.

Other titles available on request — Arabic hand gestures, Traditional Gulf Khaleegy, Ya Eskanderani and more.
