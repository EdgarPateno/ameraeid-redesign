The artist

An accomplished international performer & teacher.

With over 40 years of experience, Amera is an extraordinarily successful, enthusiastic and determined businessperson with an array of creative skills. A pioneering figure in the Australian belly dance community, she has spent her career contributing to the growth, visibility and evolution of Middle Eastern dance and culture — at home and across the world.

Known for her passion, authenticity and deep connection to the music, Amera continues to inspire generations through performance, education, cultural tours, workshops and mentoring.

Fashion designer · Cave Woman Australia

Couture for the stage.

Amera began her studies in Fashion Technology in 1985 and established the first Belly Dance boutique in the Southern Hemisphere in 1987. She has designed highly detailed costumes for entertainers, Mardi Gras icons, fashionistas and film stars.

A career highlight: hand-beading the intricate fringing on the Sydney 2000 Olympic costume worn by Kylie Minogue — alongside Arabian costume jewellery for Sass & Bide runway shows and props for productions including Moulin Rouge.

Beyond the stage

A passion for foster care.

Amera advocates for permanency for children in Out of Home Care and supports the foster carers who give so much. As a foster carer herself and an adoptive mum, she "walks the walk" — recruiting, assessing, training and supporting carers who provide a high level of care to children and young people.
