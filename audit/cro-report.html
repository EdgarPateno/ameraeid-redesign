01 — Executive Summary

The site tells a story. It doesn't ask for the sale.

ameraeid.com is the digital home of one of Australia's most accomplished belly dance artists — 40+ years of performance, teaching, costume design and cultural leadership. The brand equity is real. The problem is purely conversion: the website is an elegant brochure with no clear way to act.

Across all seven pages, a visitor who is ready to book a private class, join a workshop, sign up for mentoring, or reserve a place on the Lebanon tour is funnelled into the same outcome: a generic "send me a message" contact form. There are no prices, no booking calendar, no checkout, and no urgency — despite the site running on Shopify, a platform built to sell. The result is a high-friction path that leaks ready buyers and depends entirely on Amera manually answering enquiries.

The five highest-impact issues

1. There is no booking or purchase path anywhere on the site Critical

Every service — classes, workshops, mentoring, tours — ends at a contact form. Shopify can take deposits and bookings; none of that capability is used. Ready buyers are forced into a slow email negotiation instead of paying on the spot.

2. "Belly Dance Classes" is a collection page with no products Critical

The primary revenue page is a wall of text with nothing to buy and no visible CTA above the fold. The one embedded video failed to render.

3. A 40-year performer shows almost no proof Critical

No testimonials, reviews, press quotes, performance video, or student results. The single strongest asset — Amera's reputation — is described in prose but never demonstrated.

4. The homepage hero sells nothing High

The only hero call-to-action is "Read an article about Amera" (a PDF). There is no headline that states what you can do here, and no primary action.

5. Prime real estate is blank or broken High

The Contact and Tours pages open with large empty grey placeholder banners. The Tours page — "bookings open now" — contains almost no information and no price.

The opportunity in one line

This is not a redesign that needs new content — Amera's story, credibility and offerings already exist. It needs structure, proof, and a button that takes money. Adding clear offers with prices and a booking flow is the single change most likely to move revenue.

Method & Scope

How this audit was conducted

Each page was loaded on desktop (1440px) and mobile (390px), captured visually, and read in full. Pages were assessed against a standard CRO framework, in order of impact:

  • Value proposition clarity — is it obvious what this is and why it matters in 5 seconds?
  • Headline & messaging — does the page state an outcome?
  • CTA strength & hierarchy — one clear primary action, visible early?
  • Trust & social proof — testimonials, press, media, results.
  • Visual hierarchy & scannability — layout, whitespace, imagery.
  • Friction — forms, dead ends, broken elements, mobile.

Technical observations noted during the crawl:

  • Navigation lists "Tours" twice as a label, which is confusing.
  • The Contact and Tours pages render large empty grey banner images in the hero zone.
  • The Classes page video did not load.
  • A shop.app embed is blocked (403 / CSP frame-ancestors) and logs console errors.
  • Footer uses generic Shopify boilerplate ("Help Guide", "Friendly Support") that doesn't fit a personal brand.
  • Forms request a phone number with no context on response time.
03 — Page-by-Page Findings

Home & About

Homepage

ameraeid.com/

The hero asks visitors to read, not to act Critical

A full-width dancer image with a single button: "Read an online article about Amera." There is no headline stating what the site offers, and no path to the four money-making services. A cold visitor cannot tell in 5 seconds that they can book a class, workshop, mentoring session, or tour here.

Fix: Add a clear hero headline + subhead ("Learn authentic Egyptian belly dance from a 40-year master — classes, workshops, mentoring & cultural tours") and a primary CTA ("Book a private session"). Demote the article to a secondary text link.

The "40 years" credibility is buried in paragraphs High

Amera's strongest selling points (40+ years, international artist, pioneer of belly dance in Australia) appear as long prose blocks, not scannable proof. There are no logos, numbers, or testimonials.

Fix: Convert credibility into a scannable proof bar — "40+ years · International artist · 1,000s of students · Featured in [press]" — and add 2–3 testimonials above the fold.

No service navigation cards High

The homepage doesn't route visitors to its own offerings. A first-time visitor has to use the top nav to discover that classes, workshops, mentoring and tours exist.

Fix: Add a four-card "What I offer" grid (Classes · Workshops · Mentoring · Tours), each with an image, one line, and a CTA. This is the single biggest homepage win.

Contact form is the only conversion on the page Medium

Ending the homepage on a bare "message me" form sets the expectation that nothing here is bookable.

Fix: Keep the form, but lead with offer cards and proof. Make the form a fallback, not the primary action.

About

ameraeid.com/pages/about

Strong story, but visually broken by empty whitespace Critical

The content is genuinely compelling (pioneer of belly dance in Australia, Kylie Minogue's 2000 Olympic costume, Moulin Rouge, Sass & Bide, foster-care advocacy). But it renders as small text blocks floating in vast empty space with no photographs of Amera — for a visual performer, this is a missed proof opportunity.

Fix: Rebuild as alternating image + text rows. Add real photos (performance, costume work, the Olympic piece). Pull the marquee credits into a "Career highlights" timeline or stat strip.

No call-to-action ties the story to a booking High

The page ends without inviting the reader to do anything. The only link is "Read article" (a PDF).

Fix: Close with "Work with Amera" CTAs linking to Classes, Mentoring and Tours.
03 — Page-by-Page Findings (cont.)

Belly Dance Classes & Workshops

Belly Dance Classes

/collections/dance-classes

The primary revenue page sells nothing Critical

This is a Shopify collection page with no products in it. The visitor sees a banner ("Book in for a Private Class") followed by ~500 words of philosophy about private tuition — beautifully written, but with no price, no package, no "Book now" button, and a video that fails to load. The page title promises classes; the page delivers an essay.

Fix: Turn private classes into real Shopify products/booking items — e.g. "Private Class (60 min)", "3-Session Package", "Mentoring Intensive" — each with a price and an add-to-cart / booking button. Lead with the offer + price, then the philosophy as supporting copy.

Mixed messages: "no weekly classes" but page is titled Classes High

The copy explains group classes aren't currently offered, then pivots to private tuition and mentoring. A visitor expecting a class timetable is confused.

Fix: Rename/reframe to "Private Tuition & Coaching." State up front exactly what's available, for whom, at what price.

Broken video kills the one piece of proof High

"Amera Eid – Belly Dancing for beginners video" renders as empty space.

Fix: Embed a working video (YouTube) of Amera teaching/performing — this is exactly the proof the page needs.

Workshops

ameraeid.com/pages/workshops

~25 workshops dumped as an unstructured wall of text Critical

Every workshop topic (Franco-Arabic, Shaabi, Golden Era, Drum Solo, Lebanese Pop, and many more) is listed as a paragraph with a cryptic "level BB & above" tag. There is no grouping, no filtering, no images, no duration, and no pricing. The cognitive load is enormous and the page is exhausting to scan.

Fix: Convert to a filterable card grid (by level / style / theme). Each card: title, one-line hook, level, duration, photo. Group into clear categories (Egyptian Classics · Folkloric · Lebanese & Khaleeji · Musicality).

"Enquire for 2026" with no dates, prices, or booking Critical

The page mentions "Sunday July 12th 2026" and "minimum time applies," but there's no schedule, no location detail, no fee, and no way to reserve a spot — only the bottom contact form.

Fix: Add a clear "Upcoming workshops" schedule block with date, location, price, and a Book/Reserve button per event. Keep the topic library separate as "Book Amera to teach."

"level BB & above" is unexplained jargon Medium

A prospective student doesn't know what "BB" means or whether they qualify.

Fix: Add a short level key (Beginner / Beginner-plus / Intermediate / Advanced) and label each workshop plainly.
03 — Page-by-Page Findings (cont.)

Tours, Mentoring & Contact

Tours to Lebanon

/pages/tours-to-lebanon

A high-ticket offer with almost no selling Critical

A cultural tour to Lebanon is potentially the highest-value conversion on the site. The page contains: a heading, "September 2026 — Bookings open now," two link buttons (download brochure / itinerary), a large empty grey banner, and a contact form. There is no itinerary preview, no photos, no inclusions, no price, no testimonials from past travellers, and no deposit/booking mechanism.

Fix: Build a real tour landing page: hero with Lebanon imagery + dates + price-from, day-by-day itinerary, what's included, gallery, past-traveller testimonials, FAQ, and a "Reserve your place (deposit)" button. Forcing a brochure download before any value is shown loses most visitors.

"Bookings open now" with no booking High

Urgency language with no matching action undermines trust.

Fix: Pair the urgency with limited-spots messaging and a real deposit checkout.

Mentoring

ameraeid.com/pages/mentoring

Good message, severe layout problems Critical

The three pillars (Inspiration · Experience · 30+ years) are well-positioned, but they're scattered with massive vertical gaps and misaligned columns, making the page feel empty and unfinished. One photo appears low on the page.

Fix: Rebuild as a tight three-column "What you get" section with icons, then a clear pricing/package block, then the form. Remove the empty whitespace.

No package, price, or outcome promise High

"Contact me below" is the only next step. No session length, format (online/in-person), price, or what a mentee walks away with.

Fix: Define a productised mentoring offer (e.g. single session vs. 3-month program) with price and outcomes, and a Book button.

Contact

ameraeid.com/pages/contact

Opens with a large blank grey banner High

The most valuable space on the page is an empty placeholder image, pushing the form far below the fold and making the page look broken.

Fix: Replace with a warm header (photo of Amera + "Let's talk") or remove the banner entirely so the form sits at the top.

No expectations, no alternatives Medium

The form doesn't say when to expect a reply, and offers no direct email/phone/social alternative near the form for people who don't want to fill it in.

Fix: Add "I usually reply within 24–48 hours," show email + socials beside the form, and make "Comment" optional with a helpful placeholder.

Test ideas — validate, don't assume

  • Hero CTA: "Book a private session" vs. "Explore classes & workshops" — measure click-through.
  • Tours: price-on-page vs. brochure-gate — measure deposit starts.
  • Pricing display: show "from $X" vs. "enquire for pricing" on services.
  • Proof placement: testimonials above the fold vs. mid-page.

If you only do one thing

Give each service a price and a button that takes a deposit. Every other improvement compounds on top of having something to actually buy. The content and credibility are already here — the site just needs to ask for the sale.
